## ðŸ’¡ Key Lessons

### For Founders

**Don't do this**. While it's tempting to:
- Make up customers
- Fabricate testimonials
- Project unrealistic growth
- Fake partnerships

The truth will come out in due diligence, and you'll destroy trust.

**Do this instead**:
- Be honest about being pre-revenue
- Show real validation (even if small)
- Share actual customer conversations
- Admit what you don't know

### For Investors

**Red flags to watch for**:
- Impressive metrics with no specifics
- Customer names are companies, not individuals
- Everything is "pending" or "in discussion"
- No LinkedIn traces of team members
- Perfect retention (of zero customers)
- Exceptional unit economics with no data

**Questions to ask**:
- Can I speak with three customers?
- Can I see your analytics dashboard?
- What's your actual revenue (not projected)?
- How many customer interviews did you do?
- Can I see LOIs or pilot agreements?

### For Everyone

**The Warning**: It's easier than ever to create something that looks real but has zero substance. Professional tools, AI-generated content, and standard templates make "vibe-coding" a business dangerously simple.

**The Solution**: Demand evidence. Real customers, real revenue, real validation.
