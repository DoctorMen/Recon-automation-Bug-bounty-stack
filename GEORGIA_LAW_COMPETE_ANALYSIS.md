### **Question #2: If You Did Sign, Was It AFTER May 11, 2011?**

**If BEFORE May 11, 2011:**
- "Old Law" applies
- 85-90% of old non-competes are UNENFORCEABLE
- Georgia courts applied "strict scrutiny"
- Any overbroad provision = Entire agreement invalid
- No "blue-penciling" (can't fix bad agreements)

**If AFTER May 11, 2011:**
- "New Law" applies
- Courts CAN "blue-pencil" (modify to make reasonable)
- More employer-friendly
- BUT still must be reasonable

---

### **Question #3: If You Signed After 2011, Is It Reasonable?**

**Georgia law requires non-competes to be reasonable in:**

#### **A. TIME (Duration)**
- **Typical:** 1-2 years
- **Maximum:** Usually no more than 2 years
- **Exceeds 2 years:** Probably unenforceable

#### **B. GEOGRAPHY (Scope)**
- **Reasonable:** 10-mile radius around workplace
- **Possibly reasonable:** County or metro area
- **Probably unreasonable:** "Entire state of Georgia"
- **Definitely unreasonable:** "Entire United States"

#### **C. SCOPE OF ACTIVITY**
- Must only restrict COMPETITIVE activities
- Cannot be overly broad
- Must protect legitimate business interest

#### **D. PROTECTABLE BUSINESS INTEREST**
Must protect:
- Trade secrets
- Confidential information
- Customer relationships
- Specialized training
- Goodwill

**CANNOT protect:**
- General skills
- Industry knowledge
- Relationships you had BEFORE employment
